About me


My name is Brian Tan and I am an 18-year-old first year PhD student in Computer Science at the Colorado State University. I am advised by Professor Ewan Davies. My research interests are in using statisical physics methods in graph theory and combinatorics.

I graduated with my two Bachelor of Arts degree in Computer Science and Mathematics at the University of Colorado Boulder at 17 years old. I also have an Associate of Science degree in Computer Science from Front Range Community College at 16 years old.

Using Cluster Expansion for Approximate Counting on the Hard-Core Model in Sub-Quadratic Time

Brian Tan, Ewan Davies

We propose a new randomized algorithm to approximate the Partition Function on the Hard-Core Model. In 2006, Weitz [1] introduced a deterministic algorithm to approximate in time \(O(n^{1+\frac{1}{k}})\). In 2023, Anand et al [2] modified Weitz' algorithm by introducing the Recursive Marginal Sampler to approximate in time \(O(n^{1+\frac{1}{2k}})\). We propose that we can get the same results as Anand et al [2] Algorithm by replacing the Recursive Marginal Sampler with a Cluster Expansion Sampler.
